# Sample Pilot Material

Date: 2026-05-28

Use this as seed material for the first B2B demo and tests.

## 1. Sample Program

Program name:

> AI Tools for Business Productivity

Buyer:

> Corporate L&D team at a mid-size enterprise

Business goal:

> Help employees use AI tools safely and effectively for daily productivity, while reducing risk from poor prompting, data leakage, and unverified AI outputs.

Expected learners:

> 250 employees across business operations, sales, HR, finance, and team leads.

Target completion:

> 4 weeks from program launch.

## 2. Learner Roles

### Business User

Needs:

- summarize documents
- draft emails
- create meeting notes
- compare options
- avoid sharing sensitive data

Starting skill:

> Low to medium AI familiarity.

### Team Lead

Needs:

- review AI-assisted work
- coach team members
- create reusable prompts
- identify risk in AI outputs
- measure productivity improvement

Starting skill:

> Medium AI familiarity.

### Operations Analyst

Needs:

- analyze spreadsheet-like data
- summarize process issues
- draft SOPs
- create status reports
- validate AI output against source data

Starting skill:

> Medium tool familiarity, low AI governance familiarity.

## 3. Mock Source Input A: Stakeholder Email

Subject: AI productivity training requirement

We need a practical AI tools training program for business teams. The focus should be workplace usage, not deep AI theory. Employees should learn how to write better prompts, summarize documents, draft communication, create task plans, and validate AI output.

Please include responsible AI usage. We do not want employees pasting confidential customer, financial, employee, or contract data into public AI tools. The program should explain what is safe, what is not safe, and when to ask for approval.

We need separate examples for general business users and team leads. Team leads should learn how to review AI-assisted work and create reusable prompt templates for their teams.

Preferred duration: 6 hours total.

## 4. Mock Source Input B: Meeting Notes

Participants:

- L&D head
- digital transformation lead
- HR business partner
- sales enablement manager

Notes:

- Training should be practical and hands-on.
- Avoid too much technical explanation.
- Include exercises for document summarization, email drafting, meeting action items, and report creation.
- Sales team wants examples for proposal follow-up and account research.
- HR wants policy-safe examples.
- Finance raised concern about sensitive information leakage.
- Managers want a way to check whether learners can apply skills, not just attend.
- Program should produce a completion certificate.
- First cohort should include 40 learners.

Potential conflict:

- L&D suggested 6 hours.
- Sales enablement asked whether it can be done in 3 hours.

## 5. Mock Source Input C: Learner Profile

Audience:

- 60 percent business users
- 25 percent team leads
- 15 percent analysts

Constraints:

- Learners are busy and prefer short modules.
- Training should work for remote employees.
- Some learners have never used AI tools.
- Company requires examples to avoid confidential data.
- Final assessment should include practical task.

## 6. Expected Generated Requirement Summary

The program should train business employees to use AI tools for practical productivity tasks while following responsible usage rules. It should be role-specific, hands-on, and suitable for remote learners. Key outcomes include prompt writing, summarization, communication drafting, task planning, output validation, and safe handling of sensitive data.

## 7. Expected Assumptions

- The organization has an approved AI tool or policy.
- Learners can access a browser-based AI assistant.
- The training should be delivered in short modules.
- Certificate should require both quiz and practical task completion.

## 8. Expected Conflicts

- Program duration conflict: 6 hours requested by L&D, 3 hours requested by sales enablement.
- Depth conflict: broad business training requested, but some role-specific examples are required.

## 9. Expected TOC

### Module 1: AI Tool Basics and Safe Use

Topics:

- what AI tools can and cannot do
- approved vs risky use
- sensitive data rules
- human review responsibility

### Module 2: Prompting for Better Output

Topics:

- clear task instructions
- context and constraints
- examples and format requests
- iteration and refinement

### Module 3: Document and Meeting Productivity

Topics:

- summarizing long documents
- extracting action items
- creating meeting summaries
- checking output against source

### Module 4: Communication and Planning

Topics:

- drafting emails
- creating project task plans
- adapting tone
- avoiding unsupported claims

### Module 5: Role-Specific Practice

Topics:

- business user scenarios
- team lead review scenarios
- analyst reporting scenarios

### Module 6: Practical Assessment and Review

Topics:

- practical task submission
- quiz
- rubric-based evaluation
- certificate criteria

## 10. Expected Assessment Blueprint

Quiz:

- 20 questions
- passing score: 75 percent
- covers safe use, prompting, validation, and role scenarios

Practical task:

- learner receives a mock business document
- learner must create a summary, action plan, and follow-up email using AI
- learner must identify what information should not be pasted into public AI tools
- learner must explain how they validated the AI output

Rubric:

- prompt clarity
- safe data handling
- output relevance
- validation quality
- business usefulness

Certificate:

- quiz passed
- practical task submitted
- rubric score at or above threshold
- evaluator approval
